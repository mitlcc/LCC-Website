The Latino Cultural Center (LCC) holds three large annual events along with multiple smaller events including study breaks and game nights. For more information of what is going on this month check out our Calendar.

Cena A Las Seis

Cena a las Seis, or "Dinner at Six," is an annual LCC-orgnaized Family Weekend event where we bring MIT students and their families together for a semi-formal evening of Latino cuisine and student performances and presentations. The student groups that annually participate in the organization and execution of this event are the Latino Cultural Center, La Unión Chicana por Aztlán, the MIT Chapter of the Society of Latino Engineers and Scientists, the Association of Puerto Rican Students, Casino Rueda, Mujeres Latinas and the MIT Chapter of the Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers.

This year, Cena a las Seis 2015 will be held on Saturday, October 24th at 6PM in Morss Hall of Walker Memorial during Family Weekend.

Mes Latino

The purpose of Mes Latino, or Latino Heritage Month, is to unite Latinos on MIT’s campus and promote Latino culture to the entire MIT community. Over the years, Mes Latino has organized traditional as well as innovative events to pull students, faculty and staff together in celebrating Hispanic culture. This year, our main goal is to invite those from outside of the Latino community to join us in celebrating and learning about our culture.

¡Gala Sabrosura!

¡Gala Sabrosura!, the semi-formal Latino cultural night, is held during the spring semester. In the past, the event has been very successful, attracting attendances of over 150 guests. This year, we hope to draw an even bigger and more diverse crowd by organizing a more aggressive advertising campaign and contracting a professional DJ to play only the best Latino music throughout the night.
Please check back at the beginning of the spring semester for more details regarding ¡Gala Sabrosura 2015!, including scheduled performers.

Festival de las Americas (previously known as Fiesta Latinoamericana)

The purpose of Festival is to promote Latino culture and food to the greater MIT community during Mes Latino. To participate in the activity, guests must walk around the “festival” and answer trivia questions about Latin America (which are posed by representatives from the different LCC groups). After answering at least three questions, guests can turn in their vouchers for delicious cultural food. At the same time, the exhibitor groups have the chance to promote Latino culture and their respective organizations to the wider MIT community.
