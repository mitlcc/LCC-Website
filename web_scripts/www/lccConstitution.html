ARTICLE II: ORGANIZATION

SECTION 1: Name

The name of this center shall be the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) Latino Cultural Center (LCC).

SECTION 2: Purpose

The purpose of MIT Latino Cultural Center is to further the following objectives:

A. To provide Latino organizations on campus with the ability to unite and support each other
B. To provide a location for cultural events so that each student can celebrate their culture with the entire MIT community
C. To provide a source of cultural fulfillment by providing resources like (but not limited to) Latino literature, art work, and music
D. To provide office and storage space to be used by Latino organizations so that the organizations can maintain a working order and perform required business
E. To provide a space for students to meet with study groups or to study individually, which will allow Latino students to focus together on academic advancement
F. To provide a central location to maintain records and information regarding Latinos at MIT, which will allow Latino students to be constantly aware of the accomplishments that they have made at MIT
G. To provide an area to socialize and connect, allowing students the opportunity to meet people who have similar needs and experiences, and enabling students to form support groups
H. To organize Mes Latino, an annual celebration of Latino culture on MIT's campus, to take place each April

SECTION 3: Physical Space

The Latino Cultural Center, which is comprised of the Latino Cultural Center Lounge (W20-001) and Office (W20-028), belongs to the Latino students and student organizations of MIT. Each member organization shall respect items in the Center belonging to another organization. It is the role of the students and student organizations to monitor the proper use of the Center's facilities and the property of the Center. Paid student Desk Workers maintain office space (i.e. cleaning and upkeep).

SECTION 4: Governance

The Latino Cultural Center shall be governed by these by-laws and enforced by the Latino Cultural Center Executive Board (LCC Exec). The Center agrees to abide by the rules and regulations of the Association of Student Activities (http://web.mit.edu/asa/start/rules.html), and its executive board. This constitution, amendments to it, and the by-laws of this organization shall be subject to review by the ASA Executive Board to insure that they are in accordance with the aforementioned rules and regulations.

ARTICLE III: MEMBERSHIP

SECTION 1: Individual Student Membership

Any MIT student who identifies with, or is interested in learning more about, Latino culture is considered a member of the Latino Cultural Center. There are no membership dues. Members may join the LatinoLounge mailing list to receive event invitations and general information by visiting https://mailman.mit.edu:444/mailman/listinfo/latinolounge or by emailing the LCC Executive Board at lcc-exec@mit.edu.

SECTION 2: Student Group Membership

An MIT student group may become a member of the Latino Cultural Center at any time by emailing the LCC Executive Board at lcc-exec@mit.edu. Student group membership is permitted for all Association of Student Activities (ASA)- and Graduate Student Council (GSC)- recognized organizations whose members identify with, or are interested in learning more about, Latino culture.

Student group membership provides the following benefits to student organizations:

  1. Increased exposure: the group will be advertised in all LCC literature and on the LCC website (http://web.mit.edu/latinocenter/www/)
  2. Stronger connection to the Latino community at MIT
  3. Opportunity to be more actively involved in annual LCC-sponsored events, such as Cena a las Seis, Gala Sabrosura!, and Festival de las Americas (more information about these events can be found on the LCC website at http://latino.mit.edu
  4. Storage space in the LCC Office
The group must go through the following approval process to gain LCC student group membership:
  1. Submit a proposal to be reviewed by the LCC Exec board on how their organization works to promote the Latino community
  2. Attend an LLA meeting to present their proposal to the Assembly
A student group member is required to do the following:
  1. Have at least two of their executive board members attend the Annual Latino Leaders Fall and Spring Retreat* (Immediate Fine: $50)
  2. Host at least two open-campus events for Mes Latino each April* (Immediate Fine: $50)
  3. Have at least one of their executive board members attend the Latino Leaders Assembly meetings (Fine: $25)
  4. Add all events to the Latino@MIT Google Calendar (http://web.mit.edu/latinocenter/www/calendar.html) at least one week prior to the event date. (Fine: $5 per event)
  5. Actively participate in and assist with the three major LCC annual events: Cena a las Seis, Gala Sabrosura!, and Festival de las Americas (Fine: $25)
A student group member that does not follow the aforementioned requirements will be subject to the following penalties:
  1. First offense: written warning via email
  2. Second offense: verbal warning- meeting with the President of the organization
  3. Third offense: fine as described above
  4. Fourth offense: loss of space in the LCC Office
  5. Fifth offense: de-recognition from the LCC

* Violation of Requirements 1 and 2 are subject to immediate fines without written or verbal warnings.

In addition, after a semester of inactivity from a member organization, that organization will be derecognized.

After de-recognition from the LCC, a group may regain its membership by going through the approval process stated above.

SECTION 3: Latino Leaders Assembly

While everyone is encouraged to attend the Latino Leaders Assembly (LLA) meetings, the voting membership in the Latino Leaders Assembly shall consist of:

A. A maximum of ONE (1) student from each LCC-member organization. Each member group will be allowed a maximum of one representative. The representative must be an executive board member.

  • Each voting member must be enrolled at MIT as an undergraduate or graduate student.
  • Each voting member will be given one vote.
B. Non-voting member(s) are:
  • Any LLA meeting attendees other than the LCC-member group representatives
  • The LCC Graduate Assistant (GA): LCC Exec will work with the Assistant Director of the MIT Student Activities Office (SAO) to recruit, interview, and hire a Graduate Assistant. The GA's duties shall be as follows:
    1. To serve as the official advisor to the Latino Cultural Center Executive Board
    2. To support and be a resource to all students and student organizations affiliated with the LCC
    3. To foster community within the Latino/a and Hispanic student population at MIT through the use of the Latino Cultural Center space
    4. To generate collaboration between LCC student organizations and other student groups on-campus, both cultural and non-cultural
    5. To serve on SAO's Diversity Initiatives Group
    6. To assist in the planning of workshops and programming revolving around diversity and multiculturalism

ARTICLE IV: GOVERNMENT

SECTION 1: Membership

All legislative power shall be vested in the Latino Cultural Center Executive Board (LCC Exec). LCC Exec shall set its own rules of procedure. LCC Exec is the final authority of the LCC.

SECTION 2: Officers

A. The officers of the Latino Cultural Center Executive Board shall be a(n) President, Vice President, Director of Finances, Director of the Latino Leaders Assembly, Director of Publicity, Director of Operations, Historian, Director of External Relations and Space Manager.
B. The officers for the following academic year shall be elected in the above order no later than the end of final exams week of the current academic year.
C. A vacancy in the office of President shall be filled by the Vice President. A vacancy in any other office shall be filled by a special election for the remainder of the academic year.

SECTION 3: Officers' Duties

A. The President shall do the following:

  • Work with the rest of LCC Exec to set expectations and goals.
  • Perform monthly informal progress evaluations and amend board expectations and goals accordingly.
  • Coordinate and lead all meetings of LCC Exec, giving three days notice of all meetings.
  • Serve as the official representative of the LCC or appoint a proxy as may be necessary.
  • Prepare the annual report of the Center's activities (including the activities of its member organizations) in conjunction with the GA.
  • Serve as the official liaison between the LCC and the MIT Office of Minority Education (OME), SAO, and other MIT offices.
  • Serve as the official liaison between the LCC and groups outside of MIT.
B. The Vice President shall do the following:
  • Perform all duties of the President in his/her absence.
  • Produce and distribute meeting minutes for LCC Exec and Latino Leaders Assembly meetings and make these records available to all interested members of the LCC if requested.
  • Keep an updated list of the current executive board members of the Latino organizations under the LCC.
  • Update the LCC mailing lists (i.e. lcc-exec@mit.edu, latino-lounge@mit.edu).
  • Moderate the LatinoLounge mailing list.
  • Take charge of the revision of the LCC Constitution on the dates prescribed by Article VIII.
C. The Director of Finances shall do the following:
  • Set the annual budget for the Center in conjunction with the GA and Assistant Director of SAO.
  • Process all student reimbursements within one (1) week of obtaining receipts.
  • Pay all bills authorized by LCC Exec.
  • Keep an exact account of all receipts and expenditures.
  • Assist in event fundraising via proposal writing.
D. The Director of the Latino Leaders Assembly shall do the following:
  • Coordinate and lead all meetings of the Latino Leaders Assembly, giving one-week notice of all meetings.
  • Act as the main point of contact between the LCC and the LLA
  • Make agenda available at least 24 hours before each LLA meeting and distribute minutes to LLA after each meeting.
  • dMaintain an updated list of latino-execs@mit.edu.
  • Enforce student group requirements and take measures as mentioned in Article III, Section II.
E. The Director of Publicity shall do the following:
  • Design advertising literature for LCC events both online and on campus (i.e. flyers, emails , facebook, etc.)
  • Create plan for publicity implementation including location of postering and timeline for when different modes of publicity will be implemented.
  • Maintain MIT Events calendar.
F. Director of Operations shall do the following:
  • Reserve space for events and retreats
  • Organize food purchases for events
  • Design logistics plan for the following events:
    1. Cena a las Seis (Family Weekend, October)
    2. Gala Sabrosura! (during Mes Latino)
    3. Fiesta Latinoamericana (during Mes Latino)
    4. Study Breaks, reviews and mixers. (year-round)
  • Keep a list of things that need to be done weekly and send reminder emails
G. The Historian shall do the following:
  • a. Document Events through photographs
  • b. Organize and maintain Dropbox account
  • c. Update and maintain the latino.mit.edu website
  • d. Create and distribute monthly newsletter (Voz Latina)
H. The Director of External Relations shall do the following:
  • Establish an active line of communication with the following groups to enhance Latino student life at MIT:
    1. Faculty
    2. Staff
    3. Alumni
    4. Student groups from other Boston-area schools
    5. Boston city-wide student groups
  • Respond to relevant incoming email for lcc-exec@mit.edu.
I. The Space Manager shall do the following:
  • Hire and supervise LCC Desk Workers.
    1. Desk Worker hours will be determined in conjunction with the Assistant Director of SAO.
    2. The Space Manager will work 10 hours at LCC Desk.
    3. Desk Workers will be hired through an interview process. The Space Manager will put out a call for new Desk Workers at the beginning of each semester and will select workers using a random number generator.
  • Liaison with the GA regarding center supplies, desk worker hours, and relations with the MIT Campus Activities Complex (CAC).
  • Organize and execute LCC programming and space use initiatives while coordinating with the executive board.
  • Manage LCC office advertising space.
  • Communicate suggestions written on the whiteboard in the LCC Lounge to the rest of LCC Exec via email and at the LCC Exec meetings.
  • Oversee the scheduling of the LCC facilities.
  • Update appropriate LCC calendars in a timely manner (using the LCC Gmail account).

SECTION 4: Election of Officers

Elections shall be conducted as follows:

  • Elections shall be held during the spring semester no later than the first week of May.
  • Nominations are to be open from the third to last Latino Leaders Assembly meeting to the beginning of the elections meeting.
  • There will be a planning meeting each spring with all newly-elected executive board members.
  • Election for an open position after the formal elections are over will be held at an LLA Meeting following public announcement of the nomination for that position.

ARTICLE V: MEETINGS

SECTION 1: LCC Exec Meetings

LCC Exec shall hold meetings every week to address all business regarding the Latino Cultural Center. All meetings shall be held with a three-day advance notice, except in the case of an emergency.

SECTION 2: Structure of LCC Exec Meetings

The structure of the LCC Exec meetings shall consist of attendance taking, updates from each officer, and a discussion of outstanding issues and new business. The LCC Exec meetings shall be presided by the LCC President.

SECTION 3: Latino Leaders Assembly Meetings

The Latino Leaders Assembly shall meet every two weeks on the weeks that there is no LCC Exec meeting. LLA meetings shall be held to exchange information among the representatives of the LCC member organizations and to discuss and vote on issues affecting the LCC. All meetings shall be held with a one-week advance notice, except in the case of an emergency.

SECTION 4: Structure of the LLA Meetings

The structure of the LLA meetings shall consist of attendance taking, updates from each member organization, and a discussion of outstanding issues, new business, and the challenges that groups are confronting and how we can help each other move forward. The LLA meetings shall be presided by the LCC Director of the Latino Leaders Assembly. All LCC Exec officers are required to attend all LLA.

ARTICLE VI: EXPENDITURES

In September and January of each year, LCC Exec will decide upon the amount of money that the Director of Finances may spend on one purchase without approval by LCC Exec. Any single purchase, which exceeds this limiting amount, will be approved or disapproved by LCC Exec at a meeting. All moneys remaining at the end of the academic year will remain the property of the Center.

ARTICLE VII: AMENDMENTS

These by-laws must first be amended by two-thirds vote of LCC Exec, provided that the amendment shall have been proposed at least one week prior to the time of voting. The amendment will then be brought to the LLA and must be amended by two-thirds of the LLA.

ARTICLE VIII: REVISION

LCC Exec shall revise the LCC Constitution every third year to ensure that the constitution remains viable to the Membership beginning from this the year 2003. A two-thirds vote of a quorum is required to accept any major revisions.

ARTICLE IX: VOTING

Any issue shall be brought before the LLA and voted upon by a simple majority of the quorum.
